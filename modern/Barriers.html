Drivers and Barriers for Produce Consumption in the U.S.

This study gives us insight into when, why and how U.S. consumers choose fruits and vegetables.

By understanding current habits and behaviors limiting consumption, fresh produce retailers and marketers can change the game to break through consumers' perceptions to increase consumption.

Here's how:
  • Do the work for the consumer.
    Make fruits and vegetables more convenient to the consumer
    throughout the day with ready-to-eat, snack packs, single serve and mini-varieties.
  • Younger consumers are less habitual eaters.
    There is more of a chance to capture them with smaller portion sizes and
    mini-varieties, as well as with exotic or unfamiliar varieties.
  • Help vegetables "own" dinner at home and shine as the star of the meal.
    Move beyond promoting fruits and vegetables just as "smart choices" and "good for you." These attributes may restrict when and why consumers will eat them. Showcase the full flavor potential of fruits and vegetables, making them fun, sweet and satisfying.

Women Making Soup - A Healthy Choice Food plays both a functional role and an emotional role, which differ between lunch at work/school and lunch at home.

The full report, available exclusively to members, identifies thought-provoking strategies to leverage what is working and sought after in fruits and vegetables and to develop products, communication or packaging ideas to remove barriers.
